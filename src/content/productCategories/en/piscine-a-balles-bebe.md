## How to choose the right baby ball pit?

A good **baby ball pit** combines **safety**, **a format suited to your space**, and **material quality**. Here are the criteria that really make the difference.

### Size: compact or large format?

The size of the ball pit defines how you'll use it:

- **Square or 120×120 cm format**: compact, perfect for a corner of the bedroom or a small apartment, ideal for one child
- **120×180 cm**: comfortable mid-range surface for 1-2 children
- **150×180 cm**: large format, suited to families with several children
- **180×200 cm**: the XXL ball pit — a true collective play area

Tip: leave 30 cm of margin all around so escaping balls aren't immediately lost.

### Which balls to use?

Not all balls are equal. For a baby ball pit, look for:
- **Minimum 7 cm diameter** (anti-choking standard for under-3s)
- **BPA- and phthalate-free plastic**, no chemical smell
- **No protruding seams** or sharp edges
- **A mix of vibrant colors** to stimulate vision

Ball pits are sold as structure only — balls are purchased separately, which lets you choose the quantity and colors.

### Safety: what to check

- **Padded, soft edges**: no rigid structure that could hurt baby
- **No small detachable parts** on the structure
- **Washable fabric** or easily sponge-cleaned
- **Floor stability**: the pit shouldn't slide when a child enters

### Indoor or outdoor?

Most ball pits work **indoors and occasionally outdoors** (garden, terrace). Avoid prolonged humidity and intense direct sun, which degrade the fabric. Bring it back inside after outdoor use, and wipe it down if it has stayed out.
