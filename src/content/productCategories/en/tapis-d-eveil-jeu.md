## How to choose the right activity mat for your baby?

A good baby play mat combines three key criteria: **material safety**, **foam comfort**, and **visual stimulation** suited to the baby's age.

### EVA or EPE foam: what's the difference?

- **EPE foam**: lighter, excellent shock absorption, ideal for crawling toddlers. This is the material of our puzzle mats.
- **EVA foam**: denser and firmer, better suited to older children and intensive use (playroom, gym).

For babies from 0 to 18 months, EPE foam is the best choice: it cushions falls without being too soft (which would slow motor development).

### Puzzle format vs. one-piece mat

**The puzzle format** (interlocking tiles) usually wins:
- Adaptable to your space
- Easy to store and transport
- Replaceable tile-by-tile in case of stubborn stains

**The one-piece mat** is ideal if you want to cover a large surface without tiles coming apart.

### Colors: stimulation or overload?

Up to 3 months, babies mostly distinguish strong contrasts (black/white, red). From 4-5 months, vibrant colors stimulate vision and attention.

Our navy & pink or navy & yellow mats strike the right balance: contrasted enough to stimulate, soft enough not to tire the eyes.

### Maintenance and hygiene

Babies drool, spill, sprawl — the mat must be easy to clean. Prefer a smooth surface (sponge-cleanable) over a textured one that traps dirt.
