## Why the mouse pad is a devastatingly effective promotional medium

### Unmatched daily visibility

Unlike a pen slipped into a bag or a planner relegated to a closet, the mouse pad occupies a **fixed, central spot** on the desk. According to several studies on work habits, an employee spends an average of **6 to 8 hours a day** at their workstation. During all that time, your logo, slogan or visual identity is in their line of sight. It's an advertising exposure few media can match, with no recurring cost after initial production.

### An object perceived as useful, therefore kept

The first criterion for swag durability is simple: is it useful? A quality mouse pad meets a real ergonomic need. It improves cursor accuracy, protects the desk from scratches and, if equipped with a **gel wrist rest**, reduces muscle fatigue. An object judged useful is an object kept. And an object kept is brand exposure that lasts months, even years.

> Swag kept more than 12 months generates on average **3 times more impressions** than a classic ad placement - source: Promotional Products Association International.

## How to customize a mouse pad for your business

### Available printing techniques

Several processes exist to put your visual identity on a mouse pad:

- **Direct digital printing**: ideal for complex visuals with gradients or photographs, it offers excellent color fidelity.
- **Screen printing**: perfect for simple logos with few colors, very durable over time.
- **Thermal sublimation**: recommended for *full surface* pads, it embeds ink into the fibers for an almost tear-proof finish.

For **durable promotional printing**, sublimation is generally favored on volume B2B orders.

### The formats to choose by use

Format determines visual impact. A standard mouse pad **(220 x 180 mm / 9 x 7 in)** suits tight spaces, but an **XXL desk mat (900 x 400 mm / 35 x 16 in or more)** offers a much larger expression surface and an immediately perceivable premium finish. ## Mistakes to avoid when ordering promotional pads

### Skimping on surface quality

A cheap promotional mouse pad with a rough surface or coating that flakes off in a few weeks sends a negative signal about your brand. **Product quality reflects the perceived quality of your company.** Prioritize a micro-woven cloth surface with a **natural rubber non-slip base** at least 3 mm thick.

### Ignoring the recipient's ergonomics

Your remote-working employees or clients who receive your swag deserve a product that genuinely improves their comfort. If your target mainly works from home, think about a format compatible with a [professional remote-work desk mat](/en/meilleur-desk-mat-teletravail/): enough room for keyboard and mouse, stitched edges for longevity, surface compatible with all mouse types.

### Underestimating production timelines

A B2B order of promotional pads, especially in volume, requires **a production lead time of 10 to 21 business days** depending on providers, excluding mockup approval delays. Plan ahead systematically, especially if the swag is for a trade show or corporate event.
