## Cloth surface: comfort, control and versatility

### How a cloth surface behaves

The cloth pad remains the most-used reference in the world, and for good reasons. Its fibrous surface generates **moderate friction** that lets your mouse decelerate progressively, encouraging excellent control on precise movements. Concretely, a standard cloth pad offers a friction coefficient between **0.3 and 0.6**, depending on weave density and fiber quality.

### Speed or control: depending on the cloth chosen

There are actually two important sub-categories:

- **"Speed" cloths**: tight weave, almost smooth to the touch, fast glide ideal for competitive *FPS* like CS2 or Valorant
- **"Control" cloths**: looser weave, increased friction, perfect for strategy games or precision work
- **Standard polyester cloths**: balanced compromise for mixed gaming and office use

> A well-maintained cloth pad keeps its glide properties up to **18-24 months** of intensive use.

## Hard surface: maximum speed and longevity

### Characteristics of a hard surface

Hard mouse pads - also called *hard pads* - are made from ABS plastic, anodized aluminum or tempered glass. They offer **extremely fluid glide** with very little resistance, making them particularly prized in esports disciplines where reaction speed is paramount.

### Accuracy and skate wear

On a hard surface, accuracy mainly depends on the quality of your mouse skates. A new skate on aluminum allows **ultra-fast micro-corrections**, with virtually zero perceived latency. Conversely, worn skates glide irregularly and reduce accuracy. Major plus: a *hard pad* doesn't warp, doesn't fray and cleans in seconds.

The downsides not to overlook:

- More audible glide noise than cloth
- Wrist discomfort on long sessions
- Often higher price for premium aluminum or glass models

## Hybrid surface: the best of both worlds?

### What a hybrid pad really offers

Hybrid pads combine a **textile base** with a stiffened back or surface treatment. Result: glide faster than classic cloth, with consistency close to a *hard pad*, but keeping a discreet usage noise and some softness on contact.

### Who is it really suited for?

The hybrid pad particularly suits the following profiles:

- **Versatile gamers** who alternate between fast *FPS* and precision-demanding *RTS*
- **Creatives** - graphic designers, video editors - for whom glide consistency matters as much as comfort
- **Heavy office users** who want a durable surface without sacrificing ergonomics
