## FAQ - Razer, Corsair, SteelSeries or BuddyPad?

Razer or SteelSeries: which is the better gaming pad?+

Razer dominates on Speed glide with the Gigantus V2 (9.2/10), ideal for fast competitive FPS. SteelSeries surpasses Razer on thickness (6mm vs 3mm) and long-duration comfort with the QcK Heavy. For pure competitive gaming, Razer. For marathon streaming/MOBA sessions, SteelSeries.

Corsair or Razer for a gaming mouse pad?+

Corsair excels on RGB integration with the iCUE ecosystem (MM700 RGB, MM300 Pro), perfect if you already have a Corsair setup. Razer has the advantage on the range (more formats including XXL) and Speed surface. If you want a standalone pad without an ecosystem, Razer is more versatile. Corsair if you want full RGB sync.

Why is BuddyPad in this top 10 against Razer and Corsair?+

BuddyPad enters the top 3 thanks to unbeatable value: $25-40 for a micro-woven surface, stitched edges and exclusive designs not found at Razer or Corsair. On objective criteria (glide, durability, comfort), BuddyPad rivals brands at $50-75.

Is a Razer Gigantus V2 pad really worth $50-65?

For competitive FPS players (Valorant, CS2) demanding ultra-fast glide and perfect consistency, yes. For other profiles (casual gaming, remote work, streaming), a BuddyPad pad at $30 offers 90% of the performance for 50% of the price.

Is the Corsair MM300 Pro better than the SteelSeries QcK Heavy?+

They target different profiles. The Corsair MM300 Pro (8.3/10) is ideal for the iCUE ecosystem and the versatile Extended format. The SteelSeries QcK Heavy (9.0/10) is superior on pure comfort thanks to its 6mm thickness and ultra-precise Control surface. For 4+ hour sessions, the QcK Heavy clearly wins.
